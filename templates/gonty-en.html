Thatched and Gabion House

2014 - 2017

Symbiosis with Nature
Architecture Harmonizing with the Landscape

Harmony of Stone and Wood

Unique Architecture of the House

Thatched and Gabion House, designed by the renowned architectural firm Barycz and Saramowicz, is an expression of the quest for harmony between modern architecture and the natural environment. Located in North Izabelin, on the outskirts of Kampinos National Park, this innovative project combines traditional building materials with modern technologies. The use of natural thatch and gabions filled with dolomite not only emphasizes the ecological character of the house but also creates a unique texture and shape, perfectly integrating the building into the picturesque landscape.

Discover the Secrets of Thatched and Gabion House

Tradition and Modernity

The use of oak thatch refers to Polish building tradition, while the application of gabions filled with stone introduces an element of modern architecture, combining durability with aesthetics.

Integration with Nature

The massive structure of gabions acts as a shield, protecting the privacy of residents while opening the building to the surrounding nature and emphasizing its organic character.

Unique Facade Texture

The combination of oak thatch and gabion baskets has created a unique facade texture that is not only visually beautiful but also functional, providing thermal and acoustic insulation.

Space in Harmony with Nature

The realization of Thatched and Gabion House by Barycz and Saramowicz Architectural Office sets an example of modern architecture that respects and utilizes traditional building materials. Located in the secluded enclave of North Izabelin, the house is a perfect example of how modern solutions can harmoniously blend with the surrounding landscape. Thanks to the bold vision of the architects and the use of innovative materials, Thatched and Gabion House offers not only a unique design but also high living comfort and closeness to nature.
